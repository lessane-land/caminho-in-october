Trip
Credencial

Get it before leaving (Camino associations, Porto Cathedral or albergues). Stamp twice a day: needed for municipal albergues and, if you ever continue to Santiago, for the Compostela.

The boat

Caminha → A Guarda by water taxi (Xacobeo Transfer / Taxi Mar Caminha), 6-8 €, book online; times depend on the tide. In October the schedule is reduced: book 2-3 days ahead. Plan B: train to Valença, cross to Tui.

Time zone

Portugal is one hour behind Spain. On boat day you lose an hour at the dock: leave early or reach Oia by headlamp.

October daylight

~11.5 h of light, sunset around 19:30. Fine for stages up to ~27 km if you start by 8:30; the 31 km option needs a headlamp mentality.

Bookings

Portuguese municipals: first come, first served. Private albergues, Xunta and the boat: book online. October note: some seasonal albergues and trail bars close.

Backpack

Max 10% of body weight. Rain jacket (October Atlantic means business), sunscreen still, poles for the Oia cliffs, earplugs for the albergues.

Budget

35-45 €/person/day: bed 10-18, breakfast 5-8, dinner 12-15. Âncora seafood and Vigo oysters go under extraordinary expenses, as they should.

Apps and maps

Buen Camino, Wise Pilgrim (offline maps) and Gronze.com for reviews. Download maps beforehand: coverage on the Oia cliffs comes and goes.

Getting home

From Vigo: Celta train to Porto (~2h20, Guixar) or FlixBus/Alsa. To Madrid: direct AVE from Vigo-Urzáiz. Book before you leave.

Porto · Day 1 · Wed Sep 30

You land absurdly early — use it. Drop bags at Almada Downtown 101, coffee and a pastel de nata around the corner. Morning: São Bento station (twenty thousand azulejo tiles telling Portugal's history — find the 1140 battle scene), then downhill through the Ribeira lanes. Lunch at Mercado do Bolhão, recently restored, eat where the vendors eat. Afternoon: cross the upper deck of the Dom Luís I bridge on foot (Téophile Seyrig, Eiffel's partner — the exam question of the trip) to Gaia for a port lodge tasting; book the last slot at Ferreira or Cálem. Sunset from Jardim do Morro with everyone else who read the same advice — it is popular because it is correct.

Porto · Day 2 · Thu Oct 1 (save the legs)

Gentle day, big walk tomorrow. Livraria Lello only with a pre-booked timed ticket — otherwise skip without guilt; Porto's real bookshop soul is the tiny ones on Rua da Fábrica. Clérigos tower if the queue is civilised; the Igreja do Carmo azulejo façade costs nothing and photographs better. Take historic tram 1 along the river to Foz do Douro for an ocean-front coffee — a preview of your entire next week. Early dinner: one francesinha, split between two people — it is a weapon, not a dinner — or petiscos at a tasca in Cedofeita. Tonight: lay out the packs, stamp no. 1 waits at the Cathedral at 08:00.

Port wine in 60 seconds (for the smart one)

All port is fortified mid-ferment with grape spirit — that is why it is sweet and 20%. Ruby: aged in huge vats, fruit-forward, young. Tawny: aged in small barrels, oxidises to caramel and walnut; the "10/20/30 year" is an average age, not a vintage. Vintage: single exceptional year, bottled unfiltered, ages decades — the one that needs decanting. Order at a tasting: one white port with tonic (Porto's actual local drink), one 10-year tawny. Now you can out-order the sommelier.

Vigo · the reward

Oysters on Rúa da Pedra (A Pedra market, from ~11:00) — the contractual finish line. Casco Vello: Praza da Constitución, Concatedral, vermouth at any terrace that catches you. Monte do Castro for the panorama over the ría — a 20 min climb your legs will find hilarious. Cíes islands if the weather and your feet allow: in October ferries run mostly weekends and holidays and you need the free Xunta permit + ferry booking — check both a few days ahead. Before the 17:25 AVE: seafood lunch near the Mercado da Pedra; Urzáiz station is a steep 15 min uphill from the old town, allow 30 with a backpack.
