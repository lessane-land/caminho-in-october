Manal & Vane · October 2026

We walk to Vigo.

The Atlantic in October. Wooden boardwalks that go on for kilometres, eucalyptus hills, a river you cross by boat because there is no bridge, cliffs where the ocean argues with Galicia and loses. And us, amor, with backpacks and nowhere to be except the next albergue.

So we gave ourselves a walk. 160 kilometres. Porto to Vigo. Boots on October 2nd. Two countries, one coastline, seven-ish days, depending on how our legs vote. Anchovy-grade seafood on the Portuguese side, oysters waiting at the finish line.

You. Me. The ocean, every single day. Let's lace up.
Let's walk it together.

The boat · Caminha → A Guarda

The one reservation that can strand us. No bridge over the Miño, we cross by water taxi (Xacobeo Transfer or Taxi Mar Caminha, 6-8 €, 10-20 min). October runs a reduced, tide-dependent schedule: book 2-3 days ahead, confirm the evening before. Landing in Camposancos means Spain and clocks jump one hour forward. Plan B if the sea rebels: train to Valença, cross the bridge to Tui.

04 · Os sentidos

The senses.

Porto before, Vigo after, music between, and enough Portuguese and Galego to make locals smile.

Porto · Sep 30 – Oct 1

Day 1 · land early, live fully

Bags at Almada Downtown 101, pastel de nata around the corner. São Bento azulejos, Ribeira lanes, lunch at Mercado do Bolhão. Cross the Dom Luís I upper deck to Gaia for a port lodge tasting (book Ferreira or Cálem, last slot). Sunset at Jardim do Morro.

São BentoBolhãoJardim do Morro
Day 2 · save the legs

Livraria Lello only pre-booked, otherwise the Igreja do Carmo façade is free and photographs better. Historic tram 1 to Foz for an ocean-front coffee, a preview of the whole next week. Early night: stamp no. 1 waits at the Cathedral at 08:00.

LelloCarmoFoz, tram 1Sé, stamp no. 1
Port wine in 60 seconds

All port is fortified mid-ferment, that's why it's sweet and 20%. Ruby: vat-aged, fruity. Tawny: barrel-aged, caramel and walnut ("10-year" = average age, not vintage). Vintage: one great year, decades of patience. Order: white port + tonic, then a 10-year tawny. Now we out-order the sommelier.

Gaia lodges
Café Santiago · the francesinha ruling

Porto's most famous francesinha house. One sandwich, split between two people, it is a weapon, not a dinner, and we walk 28 km on Friday.

On the map

Vigo · the reward

Rúa da Pedra · the oysters

The ostreiras have shucked oysters on this street for generations. From ~11:00. The contractual finish line, and with the 17:25 AVE, a whole victory morning for it.

On the map
Captain Nemo's harbour

Jules Verne set the Nautilus refuelling on the sunken treasure of Rande, in this exact ría. His statue sits on the waterfront. Ending our walk at a Twenty Thousand Leagues pilgrimage site is the kind of detail we appreciate.

Verne statue
Monte do Castro

The panorama over the ría and the Cíes. A 20-minute climb our legs will find hilarious, but worth every complaint.

Monte do Castro
Cíes islands · if the stars align

October ferries run mostly weekends and holidays, and you need the free Xunta permit + ferry booking. Check both a few days ahead. If it works: the best beach in the Atlantic, says everyone who has seen it.
